# Product Brief: Tonight

**Created**: March 2026
**Status**: Closed

---

## Opportunity 

### Problem

You're free tonight in SF. Finding something to do means cycling through Eventbrite, Do The Bay, Instagram, and Google — each showing stale or promoted results. After 20 minutes of tab-switching, maybe you find an event.

But finding the event is only half the work. The rest is planning across separate domains: What will dinner near the venue cost? How long is the Uber? Is it 52°F and foggy at an outdoor venue? Is this a jeans-and-sneakers crowd? Are you actually free at 8pm? Each question lives on a different platform. The user is the integration layer, manually querying and stitching it all together.

The core friction: **the entire evening — events, dining, transit, weather, vibe, budget, calendar — is fragmented across domains that don't talk to each other.** This is exactly the kind of multi-domain coordination that agentic AI can collapse into a single interaction.

### Supporting Evidence

**Personal Validation:** A recurring weekend problem. The 30-minute planning ritual — pricing the full night, checking travel time, looking up vibe on Reddit, sequencing dinner → event → after — often kills the spontaneity. You default to the same place or worst, do not go out at all.

**Market Signals:** The event discovery platform market was valued at $4.9B in 2024, projected to reach $12.2B by 2033 at 10.8% CAGR (DataIntelo). Google "near me" searches grew 400% YoY (Think with Google, 2021). Americans spend an average of $301/month on entertainment (BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey, 2024), and Gen Z discretionary spending on entertainment is up 25.5% YoY (Bank of America Institute). The demand is large and growing — but no product owns the full planning flow.

**Research:** Decision friction kills spontaneity. The classic Iyengar & Lepper jam study showed purchase rates dropped from 30% to 3% when options increased from 6 to 24 (Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 2000). Ohio State research found that formally scheduling leisure activities reduces anticipated and actual enjoyment (Tonietto & Malkoc, Journal of Marketing Research, 2016). Couples spend an average of 17 minutes just deciding where to eat (OnePoll, 2019). Decision fatigue causes people to default to familiar options or avoid deciding altogether (Psychology Today, 2024). 

**Structural** **Root Cause:** Evening planning spans 5-6 domains (events, dining, transit, weather, vibe, calendar), each served by platforms with no incentive to integrate. Event platforms optimize for their own inventory. Google Maps doesn't know about events. Weather apps don't know about venues. No product runs these together.

---

## Target Audience

Young professionals in SF who go out 1-3 times per week and want better evenings than "we ended up at the same place again."

- **Socially active but time-poor** — wants to go out, not spend 30 minutes planning
- **Taste-driven** — has preferences but struggles to filter across platforms
- **Discovery-oriented** — values finding something new over repeating the familiar
- **SF-based** — navigates city-specific sources (Lu.ma, 19hz, Do The Bay) alongside national platforms

---

## Goals

**Qualitative**

- Feels like a knowledgeable local friend: "leave at 6:45, dinner here ($40), 10-min walk to the show ($25), bring a jacket, Uber home ~$18, total ~$85"
- One conversation replaces 6 separate searches across 6 apps
- Surfaces things the user wouldn't think to check — weather at the venue, total cost, vibe/dress code

**Quantitative**

- Planning time drops from 20-30 minutes to under 2 minutes
- Plans pull from 3+ event sources on average
- 70%+ of plans require zero manual edits before the user is ready to go

---

## Non-Goals

| Not Solving | Why |
|---|---|
| Ticket purchasing or booking | Link to the source; don't replace it. Payment adds massive complexity. |
| Multi-city support | SF-first. Event sources and venue knowledge are city-specific. |
| Group coordination | "What should I do tonight" for one person is hard enough. Groups are a different product. |
| Real-time availability | Requires partnerships that don't exist at MVP scale. Show what's happening; let users check availability at the source. |
| Mobile app | Web-first. Responsive web app avoids app store overhead. |
